I'm a microbial evolution researcher finishing my PhD at Vanderbilt University. I work at the intersection of experimental microbiology and quantitative data analysis — building Bayesian statistical models and genome-scale omics pipelines to understand how bacteria adapt, survive, and evolve.

My dissertation produced two first-author publications and an R package ([commaKit](https://github.com/carl-stone/comma)) pending Bioconductor submission. I build the tools my science needs when they don't exist yet.

I'm graduating in May 2026 and seeking postdoc or scientist positions in computational microbiology. [Learn more about my work](/about/) or [view my CV](/cv/).
